Aquatic Wild Meat

The Convention on the Conservation of Migratory Species of Wild Animals (CMS), also known as the Bonn Convention, defines Aquatic Wild Meat as the "products derived from wild aquatic mammals, birds and reptiles that are used for human consumption and other non-food purposes such as traditional medicines". It is obtained opportunistically (e.g., from bycatch or strandings) or from illegal or unregulated hunts.

Illegal and unregulated hunting of aquatic megafauna (e.g., aquatic mammals) for human consumption and other uses constitutes a substantial and immediate threat to many species worldwide. The aim of The Aquatic Wild Meat Database Project is to create a global database on aquatic mammal utilisation. The data is displayed both a as global map as well as individual trend graphs for each country. We expect the project to allow scientists and non-scientists, such as policy makers and the general public, to better visualise the global extent of aquatic mammal utilisation. It also will help to:

a. Identify understudied areas and those of greater concern.
b. Create an online network of different stakeholders working on aquatic wild meat(e.g., Google group).
c. Collect anecdotal records that are rarely published, by allowing users from all over the world to contribute to the database using a simple form.
d. Identify areas where opportunistic utilisation (e.g., from bycatch and strandings) led to directed catches.
e. Identify trends in aquatic mammals utilisation.
