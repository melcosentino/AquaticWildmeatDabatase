Mel Cosentino

Mel is a conservation biologist with a special interest in the impact of human activities on marine mammals. She collaborates with researchers and NGO's from around the world, working on conservation projects of diverse nature. Born in Argentina, she earned her Biology Degree in Spain and her MRes in Applied Marine and Fisheries Ecology in the UK. She is a PhD student at the University of Strathclyde (UK) and her project is focused on the acoustic behaviour of harbour porpoises in the wild working in collaboration with the Clyde Porpoise. She has authored several peer-review and conference papers in various topics including whalewatching impact, consumption of small cetaceans, and science-based policies. She is also a National Delegate to the Scientific Committee of the International Whaling Commission, an honorary member of the Wild Earth Foundation, a team member of Odyssea, and former Chair of the Jairo Award Committee, an ad hoc committee of the Marine Section of the Society for Conservation Biology. Mel is also an External Expert for the Aquatic Mammal Working Group of the Scientific Council of the Convention on the Conservation of Migratory Species of Wild Animals (CMS).
