Heaviside's dolphin (Cephalorhynchus heavisidii)

Heaviside's dolphins inhabit along the southwest coast of Africa, from Angola to South Africa. They are dark grey in their dorsal fin and behind it, and lighter grey in the rest of the body, except the ventral area that has a white trident towards the fluke tail. They are small, reaching 1.8m in length and travel in groups of between 5 to 20 individuals. IUCN = Near Threatened. Learn more about Heaviside's dolphins here.
