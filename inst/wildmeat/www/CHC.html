Hector's dolphin (Cephalorhynchus hectori)

Hector's dolphin inhabit exclusively in New Zealand waters around the southern island. The head and dorsal fin and the area behind it are dark grey, while the rest of the body is lighter grey, except the ventral area that has a white trident towards the fluke tail. They are the smallest of dolphins reaching 1.6m in length. It is believed that there are fewer than 65 individuals of the subspecies Maui dolphin left. IUCN = Endangered.
