White-beaked dolphin (Lagenorhynchus albirostris)

White-beaked dolphins are found in cold waters in the Northern Hemisphere, in oceanic waters. They are small, reaching up to 3.1 m and have a short beak. The colouration is similar to other dolphins, with the upper body and flanks being darker than the ventral area. The beak is usually white, but it may be a dark, ashy grey, in older individuals. They are usually found in groups of fewer than 10 individuals but occasionally gather in large groups. White-sided dolphins are taken for food throughout their distribution range.
