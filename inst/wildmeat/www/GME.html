Long-finned pilot whale (Globicephala melas)

Short-finned pilot whales can be found in North Atlantic waters as well as in temperate and subantarctic waters in the southern hemisphere. They belong to the Delphinidae family yet their size and behaviour differs from other oceanic dolphins. They are black except for a greyish area in the ventral part as well as behind the dorsal fin. They are similar to short-finned pilot whales but larger, with females reaching 5.7 m in length while males reach up to 6.7 m. They live in matrilineal groups of between 8 and 12 individuals, spending most of their time together. IUCN = Data Deficient. Learn more about Long-finned pilot whales here.
