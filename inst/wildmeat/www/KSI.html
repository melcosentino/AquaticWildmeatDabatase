Dwarf sperm whale (Kogia sima)

Little is known about dwarf sperm whales, which are rarely seen in their natural environment. They are found in temperate and tropical waters around the globe, in the continental and slope areas. They are small, reaching between 2 and 2.7m in length. Dwarf sperm whales are gray coloration, square head, small jaw, and robust body. They travel in small groups of 2 to 4 individuals or alone. Unique to the genus Kogia, is a sac hanging off from the small intestines near the anus. The sac is filled with a dense, red-brown fluid that is released when the animal is stressed.
