Chilean dolphin (Cephalorhynchus eutropia)

Chilean dolphins are found exclusively along the Chilean coast as well as the very south of Argentina off Tierra del Fuego. They are dark grey in the dorsal area, with two white patches in the ventral area, under the head and in the belly-tail area. They are small, reaching a maximum of 1.7m in length, and travel in groups of between 2 and 15 individuals. IUCN = Near Threatened. Learn more about Chilean dolphins here.
